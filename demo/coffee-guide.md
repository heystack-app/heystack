# Coffee brewing guide

## Pour over

A pour over uses a paper filter and a slow, circular pour. A good starting point
is a 1 to 16 ratio: 30 grams of coffee to 480 grams of water. Use water just off
the boil, around 94 to 96 degrees Celsius. Bloom the grounds with a small pour
for 30 seconds, then add the rest in slow circles.

## French press

The French press is full immersion. Use a coarser grind and a 1 to 15 ratio.
Steep for four minutes, then press the plunger down slowly.

## Espresso

Espresso forces hot water through finely ground, compacted coffee under pressure.
A typical shot uses 18 grams of coffee and yields about 36 grams of liquid in
25 to 30 seconds, a 1 to 2 ratio.

## Storage

Keep beans in an airtight container away from light and heat, and grind just
before brewing for the best flavour.
